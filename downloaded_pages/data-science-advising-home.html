Welcome to Data Science Advising!

Our team of professional staff and student peer advisors are here to help you navigate the many options available to you, and make the most of your time at UC Berkeley!

Contact an Advisor

Undergraduate Major Advisors can help you create a 2-year or 4-year plan, ensure that you are on track to graduate, and help make the most of your time at UC Berkeley. 

Data Science Career Advising offers individualized career services and offers classes to enhance student career readiness.

Data Science Peer Advisors (DSPAs) can offer a student perspective on choosing classes or planning your schedule, and can answer quick questions about academic policies, procedures and deadlines.

Data Science Transfer Mentors assist new transfer students with making the transition to Cal, and run our Transfer Orientation program.

Advising Resources

Visit our Forms & Procedures page to submit a form online or find out how to get an advisor signature (CPT/OPT, Conditions to Declare, etc.).

Get advice about Enrollment in Data Classes including how to clear prerequisites.

View advice for Studying Abroad as a Data Science student, including our previously-reviewed course database.

Learn more about conducting an individual research project as part of the Data Science Honors Program.

Check out Tutoring Resources available around campus for data science-related courses.

View answers to our most Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)!

Types of Advising

Book an appointment with a Major Advisor for longer conversations, like:

  • creating a 2-year or 4-year program plan
  • study abroad planning
  • academic probation or study skills

Come to our Major Advisor Drop-Ins (virtual only) for quick or urgent questions, like:

  • advice on choosing classes for the current semester
  • quick questions about requirements or procedures
  • advice needed sooner than the next available appointment

Meet with a Peer Advisor on drop-ins (virtual or in-person) for quick questions, or a student perspective:

  • choosing classes or how many classes to take
  • quick questions about requirements or procedures
  • choosing a domain emphasis (see Peer Advisor biographies for their domain emphases)
