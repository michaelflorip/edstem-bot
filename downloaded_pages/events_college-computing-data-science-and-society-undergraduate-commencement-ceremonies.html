College of Computing, Data Science, and Society Undergraduate Commencement Ceremonies

Thursday, May 16, 2024
Hearst Greek Theatre

2:00 pm Pacific Time
7:00 pm Pacific Time

We have heard student concerns and requests for additional guest tickets so that graduates can celebrate with family and friends. In order to accommodate these requests, CDSS will be holding two commencement ceremonies at the Greek Theatre on May 16.

Graduating students will be allotted 6 tickets for their guests. We are working with Cal Performances to determine ticket sales, and will continue updating graduating students via email from Marching Order with additional information as it becomes available. 

Inaugural CDSS Commencement

The inaugural College of Computing, Data Science, and Society (CDSS) Undergraduate Commencement Ceremonies on May 16, 2024 marks the first graduating class of the newly established college, joining the Computer Science, Data Science, and Statistics programs. Two ceremonies will be held to accommodate graduates and their guests:

  • 2:00 pm PT
  • 7:00 pm PT
Image

Commencement Speaker

DJ Patil will speak at both of CDSSâ€™ undergraduate graduation ceremonies.

Participate

Majors in Computer Science, Data Science, and Statistics who have (or will be) completing all coursework with an expected graduation term of Fall 2023, Spring 2024, Summer 2024, or Fall 2024 will be invited to participate in this CDSS commencement ceremony.

Registration for graduating students will close on April 18. Students should select which ceremony they prefer to participate in via their online registration with Marching Order. 

For the 2:00 p.m. ceremony, the Greek Theatre will open at 1:00 p.m. for graduates, families, and guests.

For the 7:00 p.m. ceremony, the Greek Theatre will open at 6:00 p.m. for graduates, families, and guests.

  • Families and guests should enter through the North Gate.
  • Graduates should enter through the South Gate. 
  • Families and guests in wheelchairs and those who have difficulty with stairs should also enter through the South Gate. 

Graduates and their guests should plan to arrive early. The entrances to the Greek Theatre require event security checkpoints, and there is the possibility of waiting in a line to enter.

Tickets for attending family and friends 

Graduating students will be allotted 6 tickets for their guests. We are working with Cal Performances to determine ticket sales, and will continue updating graduating students via email from Marching Order with additional information as it becomes available.

All guests (regardless of age) must have a ticket to gain admittance to commencement. Graduates must register on Marching Order by April 18 to participate, but do not need a ticket. A link to purchase tickets will be emailed to all graduates who have completed their Marching Order registration.

All commencement ticket sales are final. 

Tickets and ticket sales for the College of Computing, Data Science, and Society commencement ceremonies are separate from the May 11 campus wide commencement.

Cap and gown

Caps and gowns are available for graduates to purchase in person at the Cal Student Store (2495 Bancroft Way) and in the Graduation Department at Fall Rush (2470 Bancroft Way). Graduating students can  also purchase their cap and gown online via the Cal Student Store. 

Parking and shuttles

Public parking is available on a first come, first serve basis for commencement ceremonies taking place at the Greek Theatre. Graduating students and their guests may use the free Bear Transit campus shuttles to travel from the lot to the Greek Theatre.

Accessibility 

If you require any disability-related accommodations to participate fully in this event, please contact CDSScommencement@berkeley.edu with as much advance notice as possible, at least 7-10 days in advance of the event.

Notice of filming and photography

Please be aware that by attending this event, you will be entering an area where photography, audio and video recording may occur. By participating, you consent and give the University of California and its successors, assigns and licensees the right to release, publish, exhibit or reproduce such media, without compensation, for educational or promotional purposes and for sharing with external news media, throughout the world in perpetuity.

Questions?

Email CDSScommencement@berkeley.edu
